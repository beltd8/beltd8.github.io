Unit Circle Relationships

y/r = sin(angle)
x/r = cos(angle)
y = r * sin(angle)
x = r * cos(angle)

Using radius = 1

y/1 = sin(angle)
x/1 = cos(angle)
y = 1 * sin(angle)
x = 1 * cos(angle)

Perspective Projection

We have a point (x, y, z).

Projection is related with the correlation:

x' = x/z
y' = y/z

Projection from 3D space (x, y, z) onto a 2D plane using whatâ€™s called perspective projection.

In perspective projection, the farther away the point (larger z), the smaller its projected coordinates.

Common Principle

Both the unit circle trigonometry and the 3D-to-2D projection rely on the same geometric principle: ratios of lengths relative to a reference distance.

  • Circle: normalized by radius r.
  • Projection: normalized by depth z.

This normalization produces dimensionless ratios (sine, cosine, or projected coordinates) that describe geometry in a simplified way.

Aspect Ratio

AR = width/height

x' = x/(z*AR)
y' = y/z
  • If AR > 1 (wide screen), x' is compressed horizontally.
  • If AR < 1 (tall screen), x' is stretched horizontally.
